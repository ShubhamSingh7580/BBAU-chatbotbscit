3 Years

BCA

The Bachelor of Computer Applications (BCA) is a 3-year undergraduate degree program in computer science and information technology. It provides a strong foundation in programming, software development, and computer applications. The curriculum typically covers subjects like database management, networking, web development, programming languages (such as C++, Java, and Python), and software engineering. BCA graduates can pursue careers in software development, IT consulting, systems management, and more. It is a popular choice for students looking to enter the IT industry. The 4-year BCA Honours (Bachelor of Computer Applications) is an advanced undergraduate program that focuses on providing in-depth knowledge of computer science, programming, software development, and IT applications. This extended program includes a comprehensive curriculum with core subjects like programming languages, database management, web development, and computer networks, along with additional research, internships, and specialized electives in the final year. It prepares students for technical roles in the IT industry, as well as for pursuing higher education or research in computer science.

3 Years

BScIT

The Bachelor of Science in Information Technology (BScIT) is a three-year undergraduate program designed to equip students with foundational knowledge and practical skills in the field of IT. This course covers essential topics such as programming, database management, web development, computer networks, and software engineering, providing a balanced understanding of both theory and application. Students gain hands-on experience through lab sessions, projects, and assignments, which prepare them to tackle real-world IT challenges. The BScIT program fosters problem-solving and analytical skills, which are essential for adapting to the fast-paced tech industry. Graduates of this program can pursue careers in areas like software development, IT support, web design, systems administration, and network management. The BScIT degree also serves as a stepping stone for further studies, such as a Master's in Information Technology, or for obtaining specialized certifications in areas like cybersecurity, data analytics, or cloud computing, opening up additional career opportunities in a variety of tech-driven fields.

4 Years

BCA-Honors

The Bachelor of Computer Applications - Honors (BCA-Honors) is a four-year undergraduate program designed to give students an in-depth understanding of computer science, software development, and modern technological applications. This program is aligned with the latest industry standards, offering a comprehensive curriculum that covers programming languages, data structures, algorithms, database management, artificial intelligence, cybersecurity, and cloud computing. With an extra year compared to the traditional BCA program, BCA-Honors allows students to delve deeper into advanced topics and gain practical experience through extensive lab work, internships, and real-world projects. The program focuses not only on technical skills but also on critical thinking, analytical problem-solving, and project management. Graduates of BCA-Honors are well-prepared for careers in software engineering, data science, IT consultancy, network administration, and more. Additionally, the program offers a strong foundation for further studies, such as a Master's in Computer Science, or professional certifications in fields like data analytics and cybersecurity, enhancing career prospects in a rapidly evolving tech landscape.

4 Years

BScIT-Honors

The Bachelor of Science in Information Technology - Honors (BScIT-Honors) is a four-year undergraduate program that provides students with an extensive education in the principles and applications of information technology. This course covers core subjects such as programming, software engineering, network security, database management, web development, data analytics, and cloud computing. The additional fourth year allows students to explore advanced topics in greater depth, participate in hands-on projects, and gain industry-relevant skills through internships and lab work. With a strong emphasis on both theoretical knowledge and practical applications, BScIT-Honors prepares students to address complex IT challenges in real-world scenarios. Graduates of this program are well-suited for careers in software development, systems analysis, IT consulting, cybersecurity, and data science. The BScIT-Honors degree also lays a solid foundation for advanced studies, such as a Master's in Information Technology or certifications in areas like artificial intelligence, data science, and network security, making it ideal for students aspiring to excel in the fast-growing tech industry.

3 Years

BCom

The Bachelor of Commerce (BCom) is a three-year undergraduate program designed to provide students with a solid foundation in business, finance, accounting, and economics. This versatile course equips students with the knowledge and skills needed to understand and navigate the complexities of commerce and trade. Key subjects typically covered include financial accounting, business law, management principles, taxation, economics, and marketing, offering a well-rounded business education. Students also gain practical skills through case studies, projects, and internships, which enhance their problem-solving, analytical, and decision-making abilities. A BCom degree opens doors to diverse career opportunities in sectors such as banking, finance, accounting, auditing, and management. Graduates are well-prepared for roles as accountants, financial analysts, tax consultants, and business advisors, and the course also provides an excellent foundation for pursuing professional certifications such as CA, ACCA, and CFA, or for continuing with postgraduate studies in business or finance.

2 Years

D Pharma

The Diploma in Pharmacy (D. Pharma) is a foundational course in pharmaceutical sciences designed to provide students with essential knowledge and practical skills in pharmacy. Typically spanning two years, the program covers core subjects such as Pharmacology, Pharmaceutics, Pharmaceutical Chemistry, and Hospital and Clinical Pharmacy. The curriculum balances theoretical learning with hands-on laboratory experience, enabling students to understand drug compositions, mechanisms, and their effects on the human body. D. Pharma graduates are equipped to assist pharmacists, manage pharmaceutical businesses, or work in hospital pharmacies, healthcare facilities, and drug regulatory organizations. The course serves as an ideal entry point into the field of pharmacy, allowing graduates to either enter the workforce directly or pursue advanced studies, such as a Bachelor of Pharmacy (B. Pharma), to expand their expertise and career opportunities.

4 Years

B Pharma

The Bachelor of Pharmacy (B. Pharma) is a comprehensive four-year undergraduate program that provides in-depth knowledge and training in pharmaceutical sciences. It covers a broad range of subjects, including Pharmacology, Medicinal Chemistry, Pharmacognosy, Pharmaceutics, and Clinical Pharmacy, equipping students with the skills to develop, test, and manage medications. Through both theoretical classes and extensive lab work, students gain an understanding of drug composition, therapeutic effects, drug interactions, and safe usage. The B. Pharma curriculum also emphasizes ethical considerations, regulatory standards, and practical aspects of pharmaceutical management. Graduates are well-prepared to work in various healthcare settings, such as hospitals, drug manufacturing companies, regulatory agencies, and research laboratories. Additionally, a B. Pharma degree opens doors to further studies like a Master of Pharmacy (M. Pharma) or Doctor of Pharmacy (Pharm.D.), for those seeking advanced knowledge or specialization in fields such as clinical research, drug development, and quality control.

3 Years

BA-History

The Bachelor of Arts in History (BA History) is a three-year undergraduate program that delves into the study of human civilization, cultures, events, and social transformations across different time periods. This course provides a comprehensive overview of ancient, medieval, and modern history, covering topics like world civilizations, political revolutions, economic developments, and cultural changes. Students gain critical insights into the historical context of societal structures, global conflicts, social movements, and cultural heritage, analyzing the factors that have shaped the world today. Through a combination of lectures, research, and historical analysis, students develop strong critical thinking, research, and analytical skills. Graduates of BA History have a broad range of career options, including roles in education, archaeology, museum curation, public administration, and heritage conservation. The course also prepares students for further studies in history or related fields, such as political science, international relations, and anthropology, offering pathways to specialize in academic research or professional roles within cultural and historical institutions.

2 Years

MA-English

The Master of Arts in English (MA English) is a two-year postgraduate program that offers in-depth exploration and analysis of English literature, linguistics, and critical theory. This course broadens students' understanding of literary history, examining classical, modern, and contemporary works from British, American, and global literature. It includes the study of poetry, drama, prose, and literary criticism, along with specialized topics such as postcolonial studies, gender studies, and cultural theory. Through close reading and analysis, students develop advanced critical thinking, analytical, and communication skills, as well as a nuanced appreciation for language and literary expression. The MA English program prepares graduates for a wide range of careers, including academia, publishing, journalism, content writing, editing, and public relations. For those interested in further academic pursuits, it also provides a solid foundation for doctoral studies in literature or related fields, opening up paths in research, teaching, and literary analysis at higher levels.

4 Years

BFST

The Bachelor of Food Science and Technology (BFST) is a four-year undergraduate program focused on the scientific principles behind food production, processing, preservation, and quality control. This course provides students with a solid foundation in the chemistry, microbiology, and nutrition aspects of food, along with practical skills in food safety, packaging, and sustainable production techniques. The BFST curriculum covers a wide range of topics, including food engineering, dairy technology, food biochemistry, and sensory analysis, enabling students to understand and apply the science of food at various stages from farm to table. With a strong emphasis on research and lab-based learning, students are trained to develop new food products, improve existing processes, and ensure food quality and safety standards. Graduates of BFST are well-prepared for careers in the food industry, working in roles such as food technologists, quality control analysts, product developers, and regulatory affairs specialists. The course also serves as a foundation for advanced studies and research in food science, nutrition, and related fields, which can lead to more specialized careers in academia, public health, and food policy.

3 Years

BA Sociology

The Bachelor of Arts in Sociology (BA Sociology) is a three-year undergraduate program that explores the complexities of human society, social behavior, and cultural dynamics. This course provides students with a deep understanding of social structures, relationships, and institutions, examining topics such as family, education, religion, politics, and social change. Through a combination of theoretical study and empirical research, students learn to analyze issues like inequality, globalization, gender, crime, and social movements. BA Sociology fosters critical thinking and analytical skills, enabling students to understand and address societal challenges in a thoughtful and informed way. Graduates with a BA in Sociology are well-prepared for careers in social services, community development, public policy, human resources, and social research. The degree also serves as a strong foundation for further studies in sociology, anthropology, social work, or related fields, opening up pathways in academia, government, NGOs, and private sectors that address social and cultural issues.
